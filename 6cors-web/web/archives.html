2014 Edition

“Broad, wholesome, charitable views of men and things can not be acquired by vegetating in one little corner of the earth all one's lifetime.”
― Mark Twain, The Innocents Abroad

So, we decided to embark on a voyage through the waters of savoir-faire and arbitration. We packed our stuff, readied the kits, hoisted the masts and aligned our compasses. The Head of the Royal family of Tripura, Pradyot Bikram Manikya Deb Barma, came to see us off on this voyage!
The time had come to set sail!

Both of our barges, the Tehran Convention and the DISEC, finally went into the deep seas. Braving the waves of intriguingly complex agendas, hordes of treasure were uncovered from the sea of diplomacy, with every companion delegate putting in their own bit. Facts, ideas, opinions and solutions were put forth, discussed and deliberated upon.

Another moment of cheer for the shipmates came when we met the patron of our voyage on the high seas - the Swedish ambassador to India HE Mr. Harald Sandberg! Having been on the decks for more than four decades, captaining various expeditions, Mr Sandberg is an experienced seafarer when it comes to international diplomacy. Truly priceless were the gems of his experiences that he shared with us, and we pressed on with a renewed zeal.
"To meet and part is the way of life, but to meet again after parting is the hope of life."

After a charged final phase; wherein all the alliances and rivalries, deliberations and debates boiled down to a few clauses as both the committees were successful in reaching a resolution; the journey had come to a conclusion.

We were joined again by the Head of the Royal family of Tripura, Pradyot Bikram Manikya Deb Barma who commended us on the meaningful completion of our voyage. We shared a few laughs, and some part of ourselves. With a promise of continuing, year-after-year, the legacy that began with this expedition, IITGMUN 2014 bid farewell.
