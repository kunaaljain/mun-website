What is UN ?

Towards the end of the Second Great War, world leaders realised that it would take more than a lone hero if the world were to be saved from becoming any of the number of dystopias fiction authors have thrown at us over the years. Also, nobody wanted a Third World War as they realised that turning Earth into a pile of smoking nuclear ash is way worse than it sounds. So the Heads of States of 51 nations signed a historic charter, bringing into existence an organisation, or rather, a 'family' of organizations to these ends. 70 years later, with its six primary organs (the General Assembly, the Security Council, the Economic and Social Council, the Trusteeship Council, the International Court of Justice, and the UN Secretariat), 193 member states and innumerable funds, programs and specialised agencies, the UN has achieved unprecedented levels of cooperation in all areas of international relations. It has established comprehensive standards of international law and human rights and has in place a constantly evolving mechanism to ensure that these standards take effect. Perhaps the biggest achievement of the UN is summarised in their slogan - 'It's your world'. It is this idea that has given birth to MUNs. It is this idea that drives MUNs today globally.

What is MUN?

To put it simply, Model UN is cosplay for people who find following current affairs equally interesting as a nerd would find his comics. However, MUN conferences differ from Comic Cons in that the delegates actually do use their superpowers of research, critical thinking, public speaking and teamwork to draft solutions to issues that have kept diplomats scratching their heads over the years. It is easy to dismiss MUNs as simply a simulation of the UN framework, but that would only tell the beginning of the story. (Not even the beginning, as modern day MUNs evolved from Model League of Nations, a simulation of the UN's failed predecessor.) With the joint creative forces of innumerable creative minds from high school and college students (and increasing numbers of people beyond) MUNs have evolved and diversified at unthinking proportions over the years. Today, any council - real or fictional, futuristic or historical - that seeks to resolve disputes by means of meaningful diplomacy can be found simulated at a Model UN conference. The Best Delegate and other mentions add a competitive flair to the collaborative environment and help bring out the unprecedented from the council. If the current trends are any indication, good times for MUNers have just begun. (Because, IITGMUN).

About IITG

IIT Guwahati is the youngest member of the first generation of the Indian Institutes of Technology, the world renowned institutions-of-engineering-cum-geek-factories. It is in fact younger than quite many of its current residents. But in this short span, it has made quite a name for itself in the international learning circles. Spread over 700-odd acres of hills, valleys and lakes, the IITG campus houses 11 departments and 3 interdisciplinary academic centers all armed with state-of-the-art equipment for carrying out cutting edge research - not to mention some of the brightest minds in the country in the form of faculty, scholars and students. Apart from the hilltop accommodation enjoyed by faculty and staff that would rival most townships, the temporary (student) junta stays in 11 hostels named after tributaries of the river Brahmaputra, and are all just diverse and energetic. Brahmaputra himself is just a few paces away. The diverse enthusiasm and interests manifest in varied events round the year - most widely known being Alcheringa and Techniche, the two big-ticket festivals of the NorthEast that together bring forth the best in culture and techno-management. It is during these festivals that the campus becomes a must-visit, and is perhaps where you will meet us!

About IITGMUN

A conference predominantly revolving around policy debates and public speaking taking place at an engineering institute might seem out of place until you consider an alternate description. A description wherein an institute renowned for training students in using humanity's collective knowledge to solve all kinds of problems hosts a conference that enables young minds to take a broader perspective by playing decision makers and working towards a common goal - solving global problems. As an institute that is at the frontline when it comes to efforts at making a better world by means of technology, the power of diplomacy in facilitating the same is a cause of constant fascination for us. The values inherent in making a council successful - critical thinking, meaningful dialogue and teamwork - resonate with the ideals of collaboration in any field. Via IITGMUN we seek to give our two cents in developing and nurturing the community of believers in diplomacy as a tool for getting better outcomes as a group. One edition on, the experience in our basket is growing and we have met remarkable people and heard hitherto unheard stories that have given new dimensions to our conscience. IITGMUN drives on, with its efforts on fine-tuning MUN as a conference and making it ever better; and entering uncharted territories to let the world know of the many perks of being a MUNer with MUN Diaries.
