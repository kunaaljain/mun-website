International Atomic Energy Agency

Agenda- Envisioning Nuclear Future:Chernobyl in Hindsight.


Tens of acres of land turned barren, hundreds of thousands of people displaced, and a hundred billion dollar cleanup job - this is what a nuclear plant in Chernobyl resulted to, all in a matter of hours. The incident was a catastrophe of volcanic proportions. 5 months on, the damage is still being assessed, and the viability of civilian nuclear energy as an alternative is under question like never before. It is now up to the International Atomic Energy Agency - the autonomous organization that seeks to promote peaceful uses of nuclear energy - to decide on the future of this technology. Sanctions, safeguards and solutions are all up for deliberation as IIT Guwahati Model UN 2015 gears up for its historical committee. On the 24th of September 1986, a special meeting of the IAEA on which its very relevance is at stake. Presenting to you,
Envisioning Nuclear Future: Chernobyl in Hindsight.
