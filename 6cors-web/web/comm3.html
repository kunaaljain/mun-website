European Club Association

Agendas
  • Qatar World Cup 2022
  • Financial Fair Play
  • Reforms in European Football



    • Football. The Beautiful Game. The game that divides a country and unites countries. The game that unites the young and the old, the rich and the poor. It's the game of the masses. For millions around the world , football is so much more than just a game. It's their life. In Bill Shankly's words-“Some people think football is a matter of life and death. I don't like that attitude. I can assure them it is much more serious than that.”

    Europe is the Mecca of club football. The European Club Association is the sole independent body that directly represents football clubs at the European level. ECA was fully recognised by UEFA and FIFA in a formal memorandum of understanding, which was first signed in January 2008. ECA exists to protect and promote European club football. ECA exists to directly represent football clubs and safeguard and promote their interests on European club football matters.

    As you all must be aware, the 2022 FIFA World Cup to be held in Qatar has been under intense scrutiny. FIFA recently announced its decision to shift the World Cup to the winters with the finals slated to be played on the 18th of December. This move has drawn strong criticism from the ECA. While FIFA holds that international football must remain number one priority, the ECA seeks that FIFA recognize club football as an integral and irreplaceable part of world football, and hence demand an adequate compensation for a decision that could potentially throw the footballing calendar into disarray for several seasons.

    While the future of the Qatar World Cup hangs in the air, the ECA also has other pressing problems to deal with. Like the UEFA Financial Fair Play Regulations. These were agreed to in principle in September 2009, and were first implemented in the Premier League and UEFA Champions League since the end of the 2013/14 season. The response of the clubs towards it has been lukewarm at best. The future of the FFP remains uncertain. The ECA needs to come up with a definitive plan of action to allow the FFP to have its' desired effect, as well as discuss its' implementation over other parts of Europe.

    Another issue that the ECA must take-up is the recurring theme of controversies in UCL games. Too often games are ties are spoilt due to poor refereeing decisions and somewhat unfair practices. A mistake by an official at a crucial juncture often buries the tie. Moreover, the technology required to avoid these human errors already exists. It's just a matter of integrating it with the game, like has been the case in cricket and hockey.

    And while the ECA deliberates on bringing changes in the play, it might be worth looking into the concept of extra time in two-legged ties, and whether the rule of away goals should still be applicable in extra-time. Also, in a continent that faces severe winters, introducing the concept of a winter break across all leagues deserves consideration.

    So delegates, you are invited to join this unique committee, to deliberate and debate on the future of football. Use diplomacy to protect the interests and philosophies of your club and its supporters in one hand, while balancing the future of football in the other.
